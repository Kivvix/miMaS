Vlasov-Poisson equation

Vlasov equation plays an important role in the modeling of plasma physics, it is a non-linear transport in phase space $(x,v)$ of a density distribution $f = f(t,x,v)$ ; coupled with Poisson equation to estimate electric field $E=E(t,x)$: $$ \begin{cases} \partial_tf + v\partial_xf + E\partial_vf = 0 \\ \partial_xE = \int_{\mathbb{R}}f\,\mathrm{d}v - 1 \end{cases} $$

Dire ici que $x\in\Omega$ périodique, et $v\in\mathbb{R}$
Zoomer sur la filamentation

A high order method is needed in phase space $(x,v)$ to study filamentation.

Simplified model

We study the stability problem on a simplify model: transport at constant speed in one space dimension: $$ \partial_tu + a\partial_xu = 0 $$ where we discretize in space with WENO method [1], and test various Runge-Kutta method for the time integrator.
We apply von Neumann analysis on space discretization to study amplification factor in function of the phase $\phi$. Because of theory of von Neumann analysis works only with a linear method first studies on stability of WENO method have been done on linearized WENO [2]. It is a simpler way to study stability with finite Taylor series, and make a first estimation of CFL number [3].
We can try to apply von Neumann analysis to the full non-linearized WENO method. WENO method is sufficiently non-linear to obtain an amplification factor.

Exponential integrator on Vlasov-Poisson equation

We apply a Fourier transform in $x$ direction: $$\partial_t\hat{f} + i\kappa v\hat{f} + \widehat{(E\partial_vf)} = 0$$ of the form $$\partial_t u + Lu + N(u) = 0$$ Next we use integration factor (Duhamel formula): $$\partial_t(e^{i\kappa vt}\hat{f}) + e^{i\kappa vt}\widehat{(E\partial_vf)} = 0$$ of the form $$\partial_t(e^{Lt}u) + e^{Lt}N(u) = 0$$ With this exponential form we can use a classical Runge-Kutta method, in this context we call them Lawson method or Integrating factor Runge-Kutta method [4].

Lawson schemes has same characteristic polynomial as induced Runge-Kutta method: $$ p_{\text{Lawson}}(z) = p_{\text{RK}}(z)e^{L\Delta t} $$ with $L=i\kappa v\in i\mathbb{R}$ so stability domain is the same. Our numerical CFL study still works on Duhamel formula of Vlasov-Poisson.

The chosen one

Try a lot of time integrators to find the chosen one. We compare: $$H(t) = \int_{\Omega}\int_{\mathbb{R}}v^2f\,\mathrm{d}v\mathrm{d}x + \int_{\Omega}E^2\,\mathrm{d}x$$ $\frac{\mathrm{d}H}{\mathrm{d}t}=0$, we compare : $\left\| \frac{H(t)-H(0)}{H(0)} \right\|_{\infty}\!\!(\Delta t)$

Saturation of RK(8,6) and RK(7,6) is error of WENO method $\mathcal{O}(\Delta v^6)$

Conclusion

Our scheme :

  • Spectral scheme in $x$
  • WENO method in $v$
  • Lawson method induced by DP5 wich is embeded Runge-Kutta method (adaptative stepsize)
